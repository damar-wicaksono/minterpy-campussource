Barycentric transformation¶

In 1D, barycentric Lagrange interpolation is the most efficient scheme for fixed nodes1. Both determining the interpolant \(Q_{f,A}\,, A=\{0,\ldots,n\}\,, n\in \mathbb{N}\) and evaluating \(Q_{f,A}\) at any argument \(p \in \mathbb{R}\) require linear runtime \(\mathcal{O}(n)\). This is achieved by precomputing the constant barycentric weights that only depend on the locations of the interpolation nodes, but not on the function \(f : \Omega \longrightarrow \mathbb{R}\).

minterpy already partially extended the classic barycentric Lagrange interpolation to the multi-dimensional case. This is based on the fact that the transformation from Lagrange to Newton basis consists of structured sparse triangular matrices. Exploiting this structure accordingly to our preliminary results for the case of \(l_1\)-degree2 allow inverting and multiplying of the corresponding matrices much faster than in the general case, see 34 for similar approaches.

In summary, we expect to reduce the runtime complexity \(\mathcal{O}(|A|^2)\,, A =A_{m,n,p}\,, p>1\), for deriving and executing the transformations to \(\mathcal{O}(mn|A|)\) resulting in \(\mathcal{O}(\log(|A|)|A|)\) for \(A=A_{m,n,p},p = 1,\infty\).

Research and implementation enhancement are ongoing towards reaching this goal.
