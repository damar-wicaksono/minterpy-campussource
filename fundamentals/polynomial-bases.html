Multivariate polynomial bases¶

Multi-index sets and polynomial degree¶

Multi-index sets \(A\subseteq \mathbb{N}^m\) generalise the notion of polynomial degree to multi-dimensions \(m \in \mathbb{N}\). We call a multi-index set downward closed if and only if there is no \(\beta = (b_1,\dots,b_m) \in \mathbb{N}^m \setminus A\) with \(b_i \leq a_i\), for all \(i=1,\dots,m\) and some \(\alpha = (a_1,\dots,a_m) \in A\). This follows the classic terminology introduced for instance by 1.

Any (not necessarily downward closed) multi-index set \(A\subseteq \mathbb{N}^m\) is assumed to be orderd with respect to the lexicographical order \(\preceq\) from the last entry to the first, e.g., \((5,3,1)\preceq(1,0,3) \preceq(1,1,3)\).

For \(\alpha=(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_m) \in \mathbb{N}^m\) we consider the \(l_p\)-norm

\[\|\alpha\|_p = (\alpha_1^p + \cdots +\alpha_m^p)^{1/p}\]

and denote the multi-index sets of bounded \(l_p\)-norm by

\[A_{m,n,p} = \{\alpha \in \mathbb{N}^m : \|\alpha\|_p \leq n \}\,, \quad p>1 \,.\]

Indeed, the sets \(A_{m,n,p}\) are downward closed and yield the multi-indices being relevant when considering polynomials of \(l_p\)-degree \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) in dimension \(m \in \mathbb{N}\).

While \(A_{1,n,1}=A_{1,n,p} = A_{1,n,\infty}\) for all \(p>1\) the generalised notion of polynomial degree induced by the multi-index sets \(A_{m,n,p}\) just becomes observable for multi-dimensions.

Below the canonical, Lagrange & Newton bases are introduced. In fact, all of them are bases of \(\Pi_A\) (Eq. (1)) whenever \(A\) is a downward closed multi-index set.

Canonical polynomials¶

Given a not necessarily downward closed multi-index set \(A\subseteq \mathbb{N}^m\) in dimension \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) we consider the polynomial spaces

(1)¶\[\Pi_A =\left<x^\alpha = x_1^{\alpha_1}\cdots x_m^{\alpha_m} : \alpha \in A\right>\]

spanned by the canonical monomials \(x^\alpha\,, \alpha \in A\). A polynomial in canonical form

\[Q(x) = \sum_{\alpha \in A} c_\alpha x^\alpha \in \Pi_A\,.\]

is determined by \(A\) and its canonical coefficients \((c_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A} \in \mathbb{R}^{|A|}\), which are implemented as an array ordered with respect to the lexicographical order \(\preceq\).

The crucial point of our general setup of multi-index sets \(A \subseteq \mathbb{N}^m\) can for instance be observed by realising that \(\|(2,2)\|_1 = 4 > 3\), \(\|(2,2)\|_2 = \sqrt{8} < 3\) implies

\[x^2y^2 \not \in \Pi_{A_{2,3,1}}\,, \quad \text{but}\quad x^2y^2 \in \Pi_{A_{2,3,2}}\,.\]

In other words: the choice of \(l_p\)-degree constraints the combinations of considered monomials. This fact is crucial for the approximation power of polynomials as asserted in the introduction.

Lagrange polynomials¶

Given a multi-index set \(A\subseteq \mathbb{N}^m\) in dimension \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) and a set of unisolvent nodes given as the sub-grid

\[P_A = \left\{ p_\alpha = (p_{\alpha_1,1},\ldots,p_{\alpha_m,m}) \in \Omega\subseteq \mathbb{R}^m : \alpha \in A\right\}\,, \quad p_{\alpha_i,i} \in P_i \subseteq [-1,1]\,.\]

that is specified by the chosen generating nodes \(\mathrm{GP} = \oplus_{i=1}^m P_i\) the Lagrange polynomials \(L_\alpha\) are uniquely determined by being required to satisfy

\[L_{\alpha}(p_\beta) = \delta_{\alpha,\beta}\,,\]

where \(\delta_{\cdot,\cdot}\) denotes the Kronecker delta. A polynomial in Lagrange form

\[Q(x) = \sum_{\alpha \in A} c_\alpha L_\alpha \in \Pi_A\]

is determined by the choice of \(A, \mathrm{GP}\) and its Lagrange coefficients \((c_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A} \in \mathbb{R}^{|A|}\), which are implemented as an array ordered with respect to the lexicographical order \(\preceq\).

Newton polynomials¶

Given a multi-index set \(A\subseteq \mathbb{N}^m\) in dimension \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) and a set of unisolvent nodes given as the sub-grid

\[P_A = \left\{ p_\alpha = (p_{\alpha_1,1},\ldots,p_{\alpha_m,m}) \in \Omega\subseteq \mathbb{R}^m : \alpha \in A\right\}\,, \quad p_{\alpha_i,i} \in P_i\,.\]

that is specified by the chosen generating nodes \(\mathrm{GP} = \oplus_{i=1}^m P_i\) the Newton polynomials \(N_\alpha\) are defined by

\[N_\alpha(x) = \prod_{i=1}^m\prod_{j=0}^{\alpha_i -1}(x- p_{j,i})\,,\quad p_{j,i} \in P_i\]

generalising their classic notion from 1D to multi-dimensions, see e.g.23. A polynomial in Newton form

\[Q(x) = \sum_{\alpha \in A} c_\alpha N_\alpha \in \Pi_A\]

is determined by the choice of \(A, \mathrm{GP}\) and its Newton coefficients \((c_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A} \in \mathbb{R}^{|A|}\), which are implemented as an array ordered with respect to the lexicographical order \(\preceq\).
